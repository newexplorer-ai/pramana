Three tiers, one promise

If Pramana states it, you can see exactly where the Indian literature says it.

Every response is labelled with exactly one tier and its own colour. The product never blurs the line between a grounded citation and a general answer.

✓ Tier 1 · Corpus

Grounded in the curated corpus

Answers generated only from retrieved corpus passages with API-level citation enforcement, grouped by issuing body. The default, preferred outcome.

🌐 Tier 2 · Web allowlist

Extended to vetted Indian sites

When corpus confidence is low, search is restricted to an allowlist of trusted Indian medical domains — same citation discipline, stamped with the retrieval date.

✦ Tier 3 · General model

Clearly-labelled model answer

When no grounded source exists, a frontier model answers — shown directly and highlighted as carrying no Indian-literature citation. High-stakes dosing questions are never answered this way.

Questions

Is Pramana a diagnostic or treatment tool?

+

No. It is a literature reference and search tool for professionals, and does not replace clinical judgment.

Where do the answers come from?

+

A hand-curated corpus of Indian medical literature — ICMR, MoHFW, the NLEM, and open-access Indian journals — plus a vetted allowlist of Indian medical domains.

What happens when the literature doesn't cover my question?

+

You get a general model answer, shown directly and clearly highlighted as carrying no Indian-literature citation — never disguised as a grounded answer. High-stakes dosing and interaction questions are withheld rather than answered without a source.

What does the beta cost?

+

The closed beta is free for verified clinicians — no card required. Pricing will be announced before general availability.

Do you support Indian languages?

+

Not in the MVP — English input and output for now. Hindi and other languages are on the roadmap.

Is my data safe?

+

No patient-identifiable information is solicited, data is handled in-region under DPDP-conscious practices, and retention limits are documented in the privacy policy.
